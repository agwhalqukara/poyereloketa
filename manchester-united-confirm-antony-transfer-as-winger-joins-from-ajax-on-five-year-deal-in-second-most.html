Manchester United confirm Antony transfer as winger joins from Ajax on five-year deal in second most

September 13, 2024
blog

Manchester United have confirmed the signing of winger Antony from Ajax.

The Red Devils have been involved in protracted negotiations with the Dutch club for a number of weeks, but have finally got their man.

A breakthrough in negotiations was made on Sunday after United's third bid of around £76million was rejected.

The Premier League side eventually agreed to meet Ajax's asking price and will pay an initial £81million, with potential add-ons taking the deal to £85million.

That makes him United's second biggest signing, behind only Paul Pogba, and the fourth most expensive signing in Premier League history.

On Thursday morning, United confirmed he was officially a Red Devil and had penned a five-year deal.

Antony reunites with former boss Erik ten Hag, who had been pushing the club all summer to sign the winger.

Antony said: “This is an incredible moment in my career to be joining one of the most iconic clubs in the world. I’m thankful to everyone who has believed in me, especially my family, and all my coaches and team-mates, because I could not have got here without them.

“Playing under Erik ten Hag at Ajax was perfect for me and my development. His style of football and coaching brings out the best in me, and I am excited by what he has told me about his plans and ambitions in Manchester.

“My time at Ajax was fantastic and I will always be grateful for the trust they put in me, but now I am ready for the next challenge, and I can’t wait to join my new team-mates and play my part in bringing success to Manchester United.”

The Brazilian had made his intentions clear to Ajax he wanted to make the move Old Trafford and gave a revealing interview to journalist Fabrizio Romano just days before the move was confirmed.

Antony said: “Since February of this year, my agents came to Amsterdam to inform Ajax of my desire to leave the club to face new challenge and that some interested clubs would arrive and with them, certainly a great offer.

“In June of this year, I interrupted my vacation and came personally to inform the managers of Ajax, including new coach, about my wish to leave and that they should consider this possibility, because it was a project for two seasons.

“During the window months, meetings continued and I also received a proposal from Ajax for contract renewal. I made it clear once again: I want to leave."

Antony scored 31 goals and recorded 27 assists in 134 club appearances for Ajax and Sao Paulo, while he has two goals and two assists in nine senior appearances for Brazil.

During his time in the Netherlands he won two titles and an Olympic gold medal at the 2020 Summer Games with his country.

The Antony deal takes Manchester United's summer spending to well over £200m.

International teammate Casemiro signed from Real Madrid for £70m, while Lisandro Martinez also joined from Ajax for £56.7m. The Red Devils also spent £13m on fullback Tyrell Malacia, with Christian Eriksen joining on a free transfer.
